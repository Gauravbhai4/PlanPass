# Nambike Nakshe (Trusted Map) Scheme

Nambike Nakshe is a self-certification track for small residential plots in BBMP/GBA limits. The word "Nambike" means "trust" in Kannada — the scheme places legal responsibility on the licensed architect/engineer rather than waiting on BBMP plan-checker review.

## Eligibility

- Sites up to **50 ft × 80 ft (4,000 sq ft)**
- Residential use only (single-family or small multi-family)
- A-Khata properties only (B-Khata not eligible)
- Plot must fall within BBMP/GBA jurisdiction

## How It Works

1. Owner engages a **licensed architect or engineer** empanelled by BBMP/GBA
2. Architect prepares the plans in strict compliance with current Zonal Regulations and KTCP Act
3. Architect **self-certifies** the plan
4. Plans are uploaded to bpas.bbmpgov.in for final approval
5. BBMP issues provisional approval; final sanction follows after a fast-track desk review

## Why This Is Important

- **Architect carries the legal liability** for any violations discovered later
- Significantly faster than the full review track (days, not weeks)
- BBMP penalty for false certification: license suspension + ₹2L+ fine + reconstruction order

## What This Means for the Architect

- The architect MUST be 100% sure of compliance before signing
- Common pitfalls (setback miscalculation, FAR mistake, parking shortfall) become PERSONAL liability
- This is why a tool like Permit Setu is so valuable in this track — it audits the plan before the architect signs

## Architect Empanelment

- Must be a licensed architect (Council of Architecture registration) OR engineer (relevant degree + BBMP empanelment)
- Annual empanelment fee
- Empanelment can be revoked for repeated violations

## When NOT to Use Nambike Nakshe

- Sites larger than 50x80 ft → use standard sanction track
- Commercial buildings → use standard track
- Buildings G+4 and above → use standard track + structural engineer NOC
- Properties with any legal/title issues → resolve first, do not self-certify
- Heritage properties or properties in conservation zones → use standard track

## Documents Required

1. Owner's E-Khata + ePID
2. Property tax paid receipt (current)
3. Plot survey sketch
4. Architectural drawings (site plan, floor plans, elevations, sections, plumbing, electrical)
5. Structural drawings if G+2 or above
6. Architect's self-certification letter
7. Architect's empanelment certificate
8. Stamp duty payment proof for plan fees
