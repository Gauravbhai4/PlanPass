# Parking Norms — BBMP/GBA 2026

Parking is the #2 cause of plan rejection after setback violations. The 2026 amendments add EV-charging requirements.

## Residential Parking Requirements

### Single-Family Homes (G+0 to G+2)
- Minimum **1 car parking space** for plots ≥ 1,200 sq ft
- Two-wheeler parking: not mandatory but typically planned
- Stilt parking is preferred (frees ground for parking, doesn't count toward FAR)

### Multi-Family Residential
- **1 car parking space per dwelling unit** (DU) for DUs ≥ 1,000 sq ft
- 0.5 car parking per DU for DUs < 1,000 sq ft
- 1 two-wheeler space per DU
- **+10% visitor parking** (e.g., 10 units = 1 visitor car space)

### Commercial/Mixed-Use
- 1 car per 50-75 sq m of office/retail (varies by zone)
- 1 car per 4 seats for restaurants
- 1 car per 100 sq m of retail

## NEW 2026: EV Parking Mandate

- **20% of total parking spaces** must include EV charging infrastructure (cabling + meter readiness)
- Applies to all new residential buildings ≥ G+2 and all commercial buildings
- Charger installation not mandatory but conduit + meter readiness IS

## Parking Space Dimensions

| Type | Standard Size |
|------|---------------|
| Car (regular) | 2.5m × 5.0m |
| Car (compact/stack) | 2.4m × 4.8m |
| Two-wheeler | 1.0m × 2.0m |
| EV-ready car | 2.5m × 5.0m + 0.3m for charger access |
| Visitor car | Same as regular car |

## Mechanized Parking

- Mechanized (stack) parking systems are permitted
- Use of mechanized parking EXEMPTS the requirement for traditional ramps
- Useful for tight plots where ramps eat space
- Counts toward parking requirement at 1 stack = 1 space (typically)

## Driveway and Access Rules

- Minimum driveway width: 3.0 m for single car, 5.5 m for two-way
- Slope: max 1:10 for ramps to basement parking
- Turning radius: minimum 5.5 m

## Common Parking Rejection Patterns

1. Insufficient parking count for proposed dwelling units
2. Parking spaces drawn that don't meet minimum dimensions
3. No visitor parking shown on multi-family plans
4. Missing EV charging infrastructure (new in 2026 — most architects haven't updated)
5. Driveway too narrow for required access
6. Parking shown in setback area (NOT ALLOWED — parking must be inside the buildable footprint or in stilt)
