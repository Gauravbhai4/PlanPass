# Karnataka Town and Country Planning Act, 1961 (KTCP Act)

**Enacted:** 1961
**In force from:** 15 January 1965
**Jurisdiction:** Throughout Karnataka
**Authority:** Directorate of Town and Country Planning (DTCP), state government
**Status:** Equivalent role to California Government Code §66310-66342 for ADUs, but covers ALL development

## Why This Matters

Every BBMP/GBA plan approval ultimately derives its legal authority from this Act. When BBMP rejects a plan, the rejection notice often cites a specific section of the KTCP Act. The architect's response must address the cited section directly.

## Key Sections

### Section 14A — Master Plan
- Provides for the preparation and adoption of Master Plans by planning authorities
- Bengaluru's current Master Plan: Revised Master Plan (RMP) 2015 with 2026 zonal amendments
- RMP 2031 is in draft form

### Section 14B — Outline Development Plan (ODP)
- Defines land-use zones (R, RM-1, RM-2, C, I, PSP, TR)
- Any building plan must conform to the ODP zoning

### Section 15 — Sanction of Layouts
- Layout plans (subdivision of land) require planning authority sanction
- Cannot construct on unsanctioned layouts (often the underlying cause of B-Khata)

### Section 17 (Amended)
- **CRITICAL**: Individual plots now require approval from the competent planning authority to be eligible for A-Khata
- This amendment ties khata issuance directly to planning compliance
- Practical impact: BBMP will not process building plans without valid A-Khata, and A-Khata won't be issued without §17 compliance

### Section 18 — Use of Land and Buildings
- Restricts use to that permitted under the ODP
- Commercial activity in a residential zone = violation under §18

### Section 19 — Approval of Plans
- Authorizes planning authorities (BBMP, GBA, BDA) to approve or reject building plans
- Sets the legal basis for plan scrutiny

### Section 24 — Powers of Authority
- Administrative powers including the ability to delegate approval to subordinate officers
- Recent amendments improved single-window operation

### Section 76 — Penalty for Unauthorized Development
- Penalties for construction without sanctioned plan
- Includes fines and demolition orders
- Compound-able under specific provisions

## Practical Citation Patterns in BBMP Rejection Letters

| Cited Section | Typical Meaning | Architect Response |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------|
| §14B / ODP violation | Land use not permitted | Revise to permitted use OR apply for change of land use (CLU) |
| §17 | Khata/planning authority approval missing | Resolve khata first; cite valid A-Khata in response |
| §18 | Use of building doesn't match zone | Revise use; commercial in residential zone won't fly |
| §19 | Plan technical defect | Cite the specific bylaw the architect now conforms to |
| §76 | Pre-existing unauthorized construction on plot | Compounding application required before fresh approval |

## Companion Legislation

- **National Building Code (NBC) of India 2016** — referenced for fire safety, structural, accessibility
- **Bangalore Mahanagara Palike Building Bye-Laws 2003** (amended) — operational rules under KTCP Act
- **Karnataka Land Revenue Act** — property classification
- **Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act 2016 (RERA)** — applies to projects with 8+ apartments or 500+ sq m

## Drafting Tips for Corrections Response Letters

1. Always cite the specific KTCP Act section that the response addresses
2. Reference the 2026 amended Zonal Regulations notification by date (5-6 January 2026)
3. Attach proof of compliance (e.g., revised drawing sheet number + clause)
4. For Nambike Nakshe responses: include the architect's empanelment certificate number
